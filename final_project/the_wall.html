Pink Floyd's The Wall

The Wall tells the story of Pink, a character loosely based on Waters himself. The album chronicles Pink's descent into isolation and madness, symbolized by the metaphorical “wall” he builds around himself. The story explores themes like:

  1. Abandonment (his father dies in WWII)
  2. Overprotective parenting
  3. Trauma from school (harsh teachers)
  4. Failed relationships
  5. The trappings of fame
  6. Mental breakdown and eventual self-judgment
  7. The narrative ends with Pink putting himself on trial and ultimately tearing down the wall he built.
