"The Wall" is a rock opera that tells the story of Pink, a fictional rock star who becomes increasingly isolated from the world, ultimately building a metaphorical wall around himself. It's deeply personal, drawing from Roger Waters' own life, including the loss of his father in WWII, an overprotective mother, abusive school system, and the alienation caused by fame. Key Themes: Isolation War and loss Childhood trauma Mental breakdown Critique of authoritarianism (especially in education)

    🎶 Famous Songs from The Wall
  • “Another Brick in the Wall, Part 2” – with the famous line “We don’t need no education…”
  • “Comfortably Numb” – iconic guitar solos and haunting lyrics.
  • “Hey You” – a cry from inside the metaphorical wall.
  • “Run Like Hell” – intense and angry, reflecting Pink’s descent into fascist fantasy.
