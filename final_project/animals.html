Animals

Animals is a studio album by the English rock band Pink Floyd, released on January 23, 1977. It’s one of their most politically charged and concept-driven albums, known for its dark, satirical take on society. Key Details: Concept: Loosely based on George Orwell's Animal Farm, the album presents a dystopian view of modern society. People are categorized into three animal types: Dogs – representing ruthless businessmen and corporate power Pigs – symbolizing corrupt political leaders Sheep – the blind followers of the system Tracklist: Pigs on the Wing (Part 1) Dogs Pigs (Three Different Ones) Sheep Pigs on the Wing (Part 2) Sound: It features long, progressive tracks with extended instrumental sections, heavy use of synthesizers, and biting lyrics. Compared to The Dark Side of the Moon or Wish You Were Here, it’s more aggressive and less polished — intentionally so. Band Lineup: Roger Waters – bass, lead vocals, concept David Gilmour – guitar, vocals Richard Wright – keyboards Nick Mason – drums Cover Art: Designed by Hipgnosis and Roger Waters, it features the now-iconic image of a pig floating between two chimneys at the Battersea Power Station in London.
